## Hero Section

**Title:** How I Work

**Subtitle:** I design and build software products — from early strategy through production-ready components. The process below is how I consistently get there: a proven workflow refined over years of working with startups and product teams, paired with a clear engagement model so you always know what to expect. No black boxes, no handoff gaps, no surprises.

---

## Design Process

### Step 1: Uncover

**Description:**
Great product work starts by surfacing what others overlook. I dig into your business goals, user needs, and existing product ecosystem — including your design system maturity and front-end stack — so I can identify where focused design and engineering effort will move the needle fastest. The result is a shared understanding of priorities and a clear path to execution.

**Chips:**
- Product & UX Audit
- Stakeholder Alignment
- User & Market Research
- Design System Review
- Codebase Assessment

---

### Step 2: Explore

**Description:**
With priorities locked in, I move fast into divergent thinking — generating concepts, testing layout strategies, and pressure-testing ideas against real user scenarios. I use AI-augmented workflows to rapidly produce and evaluate design directions, which means I can put more options on the table in less time without sacrificing quality or intent. You see real thinking early, not polished designs weeks later.

**Chips:**
- Concept Generation
- AI-Assisted Ideation
- Wireframing & Layouts
- Interaction Modeling
- Content Strategy

---

### Step 3: Craft

**Description:**
This is where design becomes product. I build high-fidelity interfaces and production-ready front-end components in parallel, closing the gap between what's designed and what's shipped. By working across Figma and code, I eliminate the traditional handoff bottleneck — engineering receives components they can integrate directly, not specs they need to reinterpret.

**Chips:**
- High-Fidelity UI Design
- Production-Ready Components
- Design System Development
- Responsive Implementation
- Dev Handoff

---

### Step 4: Validate

**Description:**
Design isn't done when it looks right — it's done when it works right. I test interfaces with real users and real data, measuring usability, performance, and alignment with the business goals we set in the Uncover phase. Because I deliver production-ready components, validation happens against actual product behavior, not clickable prototypes — which means the insights are sharper and the iteration cycles are shorter.

**Chips:**
- Usability Testing
- Accessibility Audits
- Analytics Review
- Iterative Refinement
